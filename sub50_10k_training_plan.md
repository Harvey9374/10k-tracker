# Sub-50 Minute 10km Training Plan
## Age 42 | Start: May 2026 | Target Race: Mid-May 2027

---

## Objective

Drop from a ~59-61 min flat equivalent to sub-50 min over 52 weeks. That is 5:00/km average pace. You have done 42-45 min before, so the physiological ceiling is not the problem. Consistency and structured load is.

---

## Equipment

- 2 x 15kg dumbbells
- Ropeless skipping rope
- Outdoor running

---

## Weekly Schedule Framework

| Day | Availability | Session Type |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | 90 mins | Key run + optional strength |
| Tuesday | 90 mins | Strength + conditioning |
| Wednesday | Limited, home | Skip + mobility or light strength |
| Thursday | Limited, home | Mobility or light strength |
| Friday | 60 mins | Run session |
| Weekend (alt) | Variable | Bonus long run |

---

## Pace Guide (These evolve across phases)

| Session Type | Phase 1 | Phase 2 | Phase 3 | Phase 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Easy run | 6:30-7:00/km | 6:15-6:45/km | 6:00-6:30/km | 6:00-6:30/km |
| Steady/tempo | N/A | 5:40-5:55/km | 5:20-5:35/km | 5:05-5:20/km |
| Interval reps | N/A | 5:10-5:25/km | 4:55-5:10/km | 4:45-5:00/km |
| Race pace | N/A | N/A | 5:05/km | 5:00/km |

Easy runs should feel genuinely easy. You should be able to hold a full conversation. If you are breathing hard, slow down.

## Phase 1: Base Building
### Weeks 1-12 | June - August 2026

The goal here is to rebuild consistent volume, repair any deconditioning from reduced training, and protect the shins through gradual load increase. No hard sessions until Week 7. Patience here pays off later.

**Weekly running target:** 18-28km by end of Phase 1
**Strength focus:** Single-leg stability, calf strength, hip work

---

### Phase 1 Weekly Template

**Monday (90 mins)**
- Warm-up: 5 min walk + 5 min dynamic movement
- Easy run: 40-50 mins (Weeks 1-6), 50-60 mins (Weeks 7-12)
- Strength circuit: 15-20 mins (see Phase 1 strength below)
- Cool-down: 5 min walk + calf stretch

**Tuesday (90 mins)**
- Warm-up: 5 min ropeless skip at easy pace
- Strength session: 50-60 mins (see Phase 1 strength below)
- Skip finisher: 10 mins varied pace
- Cool-down and stretch: 10 mins

**Wednesday (home, limited)**
- Ropeless skip: 15 mins steady
- Calf raises: 3 x 20 bodyweight
- Glute bridges: 3 x 15
- Mobility: 10 mins hip flexors, hamstrings, calves

**Thursday (home, limited)**
- Foam roll or static stretch: 15-20 mins
- Breathing and recovery focus

**Friday (60 mins)**
- Warm-up: 5 mins
- Easy run: 30-40 mins
- Cool-down: 5 mins

**Weekend (alternate weeks)**
- Easy run: 40-50 mins, genuinely easy effort

---

### Phase 1 Strength Circuit

Perform as a circuit. Rest 60-90 seconds between rounds. 3 rounds total.

**A. Lower body (injury prevention focus)**
1. Single-leg Romanian deadlift: 10 reps each leg x 15kg per hand
2. Bulgarian split squat: 10 reps each leg x 15kg per hand
3. Single-leg calf raise: 15 reps each leg (use step if available), add 15kg for progression
4. Lateral band walk or lateral lunge: 12 reps each direction (bodyweight)
5. Glute bridge with hold: 10 reps, 2 second hold at top

**B. Upper body and core (supports running posture)**
1. Dumbbell bent-over row: 12 reps x 15kg each
2. Press-ups: max reps, controlled
3. Dumbbell overhead press: 10 reps x 15kg each (or combined 30kg load with caution)
4. Plank: 45-60 second hold
5. Dead bug: 10 reps each side

**Notes:** The 2 x 15kg is limiting for lower body compound work. Compensate by increasing reps (15-20 range) and slowing the tempo. 3-second lowering phase on split squats and RDLs turns 15kg into a genuine stimulus.

---

### Phase 1 Skip Protocol (Wed and warm-ups)

Ropeless skipping is ideal for low-impact cardiovascular work and coordination.

- Weeks 1-4: 15 mins steady, moderate pace
- Weeks 5-8: 15 mins with 3 x 1-min faster intervals in the middle
- Weeks 9-12: 20 mins with 5 x 1-min faster intervals

---

## Phase 2: Development
### Weeks 13-24 | September - November 2026

You now introduce quality sessions. Two key runs per week. One easy, one with structured effort. This is where fitness begins to shift noticeably.

**Weekly running target:** 28-38km by end of Phase 2
**Strength focus:** Maintain lower body work, add power

---

### Phase 2 Weekly Template

**Monday (90 mins)**
- Warm-up: 10 mins easy jog
- Tempo run: 20-30 mins at steady/tempo pace (see pace guide)
- Cool-down jog: 10 mins easy
- Optional: 15 mins strength accessory work

**Tuesday (90 mins)**
- Full strength session: 60 mins (Phase 2 strength below)
- Skip finisher: 15 mins including 5 x 1-min hard efforts

**Wednesday (home, limited)**
- Skip intervals: 20 mins (3 x 3-min moderate, 2 x 1-min hard)
- Single-leg calf raises and glute work: 15 mins

**Thursday (home, limited)**
- Mobility and stretching: 20 mins

**Friday (60 mins)**
- Warm-up: 5 mins
- Easy to steady run: 40-45 mins
- Cool-down: 5 mins

**Weekend (alternate weeks)**
- Long run: 55-70 mins at easy pace

---

### Phase 2 Strength Circuit

3-4 rounds. Rest 60-90 seconds between rounds.

1. Bulgarian split squat: 12 reps each leg x 15kg, 3-second lower
2. Single-leg RDL: 10 reps each leg x 15kg each hand
3. Dumbbell goblet squat: 15 reps x 15kg, explosive up
4. Explosive step-up (onto step or kerb): 12 reps each leg, 15kg optional
5. Single-leg calf raise on step: 20 reps each leg, 15kg held
6. Bent-over row: 12 reps x 15kg each
7. Press-ups: max controlled reps
8. Plank variations: 3 x 45-60 sec

---

### Phase 2 Interval Introduction (from Week 18)

Replace the Monday tempo run with intervals twice per month from Week 18.

Interval session structure:
- 10 min warm-up jog
- 6 x 800m at interval pace (see pace guide) with 90-second easy jog recovery
- 10 min cool-down jog

Progress to 8 x 800m by end of Phase 2.

## Phase 3: Specific Conditioning
### Weeks 25-36 | December 2026 - February 2027

Sessions now target 10km race conditions. Pace work becomes sharper. Volume stays controlled because of your time constraints, but quality is high.

**Weekly running target:** 32-42km
**Key goal:** Get comfortable at 5:05-5:10/km for extended periods

---

### Phase 3 Weekly Template

**Monday (90 mins)**
- Interval session: 10 min warm-up, main set, 10 min cool-down
- Main set options (rotate weekly):
  - 8 x 1km at 4:55-5:05/km with 90-second recovery
  - 5 x 1.5km at 5:05-5:10/km with 2-minute recovery
  - 12 x 400m at 4:45-4:55/km with 60-second recovery

**Tuesday (90 mins)**
- Strength: 45 mins (maintain, do not increase load)
- Easy run: 20-25 mins after strength

**Wednesday (home, limited)**
- Skip intervals: 25 mins including 6 x 1-min hard efforts
- Lower body stability work: 15 mins

**Thursday (home, limited)**
- Mobility and soft tissue work: 20-25 mins
- Focus on calves, hip flexors, hamstrings

**Friday (60 mins)**
- Warm-up: 5 mins
- Steady run at 5:20-5:35/km: 35-40 mins
- Cool-down: 5 mins

**Weekend (alternate weeks)**
- Long run: 65-75 mins easy pace

---

### Phase 3 Tempo Progression

Friday steady runs should progress across Phase 3:

- Weeks 25-28: 35 mins at 5:30-5:40/km
- Weeks 29-32: 35 mins at 5:20-5:30/km
- Weeks 33-36: 40 mins at 5:10-5:20/km

This is your benchmark. If Friday runs start feeling comfortable at 5:15/km, sub-50 is on track.

---

## Phase 4: Peak and Taper
### Weeks 37-50 | March - early May 2027

The final push. Sessions sharpen to race-specific pace. The taper (Weeks 49-52) reduces volume but keeps intensity so you arrive at the start line sharp, not flat.

**Weekly running target:** 35-45km (Weeks 37-46), dropping to 20km then 10km in taper

---

### Phase 4 Weekly Template (Weeks 37-46)

**Monday (90 mins)**
- Race pace session: 10 min warm-up, then:
  - 3 x 3km at 5:00-5:05/km with 3-minute recovery
  - Or: 2 x 4km at 5:00/km with 4-minute recovery

**Tuesday (90 mins)**
- Strength: reduced to 35 mins, lighter emphasis
- Easy run: 30 mins

**Wednesday (home)**
- Skip: 20 mins easy
- Mobility: 15 mins

**Thursday (home)**
- Mobility and rest focus

**Friday (60 mins)**
- Steady run: 40 mins at 5:10-5:20/km

**Weekend (alternate)**
- Easy long run: 60 mins

---

### Taper (Weeks 47-52)

**Weeks 47-48:** Cut volume by 30%. Keep all sessions but shorten runs by 20-25%. Maintain intensity.

**Week 49:** Cut volume by 50%. Easy runs only (30 mins max). One short interval session: 5 x 400m at race pace.

**Week 50:** Three easy runs of 20-25 mins. No strength work.

**Race week:** Two easy 15-min jogs early in the week. Rest Thursday and Friday. Race Saturday or Sunday.

Do not do anything new in race week. No new stretching routines, no diet changes, no new kit.

---

## Shin Splint Management

Given your history, apply these rules throughout:

1. If shin pain returns at any point, drop running volume by 50% for one week and substitute skip and bike/swim if accessible.
2. Never increase weekly running distance by more than 10% in a single week.
3. Calf raises are non-negotiable. They protect the tibialis anterior. Do them on Wednesday and Thursday every week.
4. Run on softer surfaces when possible in Phase 1 and 2.
5. If pain returns in Phase 3 or 4, rest two days then reassess. Do not push through shin pain.

Use these to check whether the plan is working:

| Checkpoint | Week | Target |
|---|---|---|
| First easy 5km time trial | Week 8 | Under 27 mins |
| 5km time trial | Week 20 | Under 25 mins |
| Solo 10km (flat) | Week 30 | Under 55 mins |

Run each time trial properly, with a warm-up, on a measured flat route. Treat them as mini-races.

## Key Principles

1. Easy runs must actually be easy. Most people run them too fast, which blunts the quality sessions.
2. The strength work is not optional. Single-leg stability directly reduces re-injury risk and improves running economy.
3. Sleep and nutrition matter as much as the sessions. At 42, recovery is slower than your mid-30s.
4. Consistency over any individual session. A 40-minute run completed beats a 70-minute run skipped.
5. If you miss a session, do not try to make it up. Move on to the next scheduled session.
6. On alternate weekends where you cannot train, it does not derail the plan. The structure absorbs it.

---

## Nutrition Notes (Brief)

No radical changes needed. Three points matter:

1. Eat enough protein. At 42 and training 4-5 times per week, aim for around 150-160g per day. Chicken, eggs, Greek yoghurt, and protein shakes cover this easily.
2. Do not run fasted in Phase 3 and 4 quality sessions. A small meal 90 mins before hard sessions improves output.
3. Hydration. Consistent daily water intake matters more than trying to optimise race-day hydration.
